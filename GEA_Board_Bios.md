# GEA Executive Board Bios

**Current Board (Elected February 2026)**

---

## Sacha Fraiture — Chairperson

Sacha Fraiture is the Public Affairs Officer (PAO) in Gaborone, Botswana. Over the past year, he was the PAO and Consular Officer in Bangui, Central African Republic (CAR). Before CAR, he was the Regional Public Engagement Specialist (REPS) based in Jordan but responsible for all American Spaces in the Middle East. Previous tours included Deputy Embassy Spokesperson in Morocco, Embassy Spokesperson in Qatar and Consular Officer in El Salvador. Before joining the State Department in 2011, Sacha spent six years as a Foreign Service Officer with USAID; managing projects in education, governance, and civil society. These postings included Egypt, USAID Representative on Provincial Reconstruction Teams in Dhi Qar, Muthanna, and Maysan provinces, Iraq, and Senegal. Sacha worked several years on security related issues; first as a Special Agent with the Diplomatic Security Service, then later as an Intelligence Consultant with Booz Allen Hamilton. He also taught secondary English, History, and governance courses in public and private schools for six years in the Washington, D.C. area. Sacha is a native of Maryland. He has a BA in History from Rutgers University and an MA in International Affairs from American University in Washington, D.C. He speaks Arabic, Spanish, and French and is an avid soccer supporter, coach, and player. He met his wife, Tara, while they were both Peace Corps Volunteers in northern Cameroon. They have three daughters.

## Michael Raney — Treasurer

Michael Raney is the Operations Management Specialist for the Deputy Chief of Mission of Embassy Gaborone. In 2025, he celebrated 20 years in the Foreign Service. Michael has served as Treasurer of the board since April 2025. Michael and his wife Kristen arrived to post with their 5 children in 2024, and have become quite the masters of mixing up some great ice cream concoctions, among their other side quests. We are grateful for Michael for staying on as Treasurer, but are also still recruiting for another USDH to serve on the board when his term expires shortly.

## Maria Ester Becerro — Secretary

Maria Ester Becerro arrived at post in July 2025 with her husband, USDH Thomas Cecil. Ester most recently served as Community Liaison Officer (CLO) at the U.S. Embassy in Uruguay (2022-2025), where she participated on the Country Team, Housing Board, and Employee Association. In her CLO role, Maria managed arrivals and orientation programming, coordinated major embassy events including the 4th of July and holiday celebrations, and oversaw employee recognition activities and happy hours. She prepared reports to Washington on education, crisis management, and quality of life issues, while supervising staff and managing administrative operations.
