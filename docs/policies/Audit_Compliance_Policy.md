# **GEA AUDIT & COMPLIANCE POLICY**
Gaborone Employee Association
**Last Updated:** March 3, 2026
Effective: March 3, 2026
## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes audit and compliance requirements for GEA operations. The policy covers logging of activities, audit trail maintenance, compliance with federal regulations (6 FAM 500-546), and Board oversight of compliance.
## 2. Audit Logging
### What Gets Logged
Membership applications: Date submitted, applicant, category assigned, status changes
Approvals: Who approved, when, what was approved, approval reason if applicable
Payments: Amount, method, date, verification status, who verified
Access changes: Who accessed what data, when, for what purpose
Data modifications: What was changed, by whom, when, from what to what
System events: Logins, logouts, errors, backups, security incidents
Document submissions and verifications: Which documents submitted, verified by whom, when
### Audit Trail Storage
All logs stored in GEA Shared Drive in 'Audit & Logs' folder
Logs maintained in read-only format (cannot be modified after creation)
Google Workspace automatically timestamps all Shared Drive activities
Treasurer maintains separate encrypted log of payment activities
Board maintains log of all Board decisions and approvals
## 3. Retention of Audit Records
Audit logs are retained indefinitely to support regulatory compliance and dispute resolution.
Approval logs: Retained indefinitely
Payment logs: Retained minimum 7 years (financial audit requirements)
Access logs: Retained indefinitely (compliance with 6 FAM 500-546)
Data modification logs: Retained indefinitely
Security incident logs: Retained indefinitely
Board decision logs: Retained indefinitely
## 4. Federal Regulation Compliance (6 FAM 500-546)
### Regulatory Requirements GEA Follows
6 FAM 500: Authority to establish association and require membership
6 FAM 542: Direct-hire employee membership (Full members)
6 FAM 544: Host country national membership (Affiliate members)
6 FAM 545: Diplomatic mission membership (Diplomatic members)
6 FAM 546: Guest policy and security requirements
6 FAM 538: Voting and board eligibility rules
### GEA Compliance Measures
Membership categories defined per federal guidelines (not per local definition alone)
Voting restricted to Full members per 6 FAM 538
Board positions limited to Full members per 6 FAM 538
Guest invitations require Full member sponsorship and RSO acknowledgment per 6 FAM 546(b)
Affiliate membership capped per Board decision with Principal Officer approval per 6 FAM 544(a)
All approvals and decisions documented and retained indefinitely
### Pending Regulatory Clarifications
Community/Guest membership regulatory basis: Clarification requested from State Department
Guest invitation authority for non-Full members: Clarification requested from State Department
See GEA_MEMBERSHIP_ACCESS_REGULATORY_ANALYSIS.md for details
Board will update policies upon State Department response
## 5. Quarterly Audit Review
Board conducts quarterly audit reviews to verify compliance and system integrity.
### Quarterly Review Checklist
Review membership applications: Approved per policy? Correct category assigned?
Review payment records: All payments verified? Payment status current?
Review reservation approvals: Excess bookings approved correctly? Bumping protocols followed?
Review facility access status: Suspended members have no access? Activated members have access?
Review security incidents: Any breaches or incidents? Documented and resolved?
Verify data integrity: All required fields populated? No missing documents? No orphaned records?
Test backup restoration: Restore sample data to verify backup integrity
Review regulatory compliance: Policies aligned with 6 FAM 500-546? Any pending changes?
### Audit Report
Board documents quarterly review findings
Report includes: Items reviewed, findings, issues identified, actions taken
Report signed by Board members conducting review
Report retained in Audit & Logs folder indefinitely
## 6. Compliance Violations
### Member Violations
Unauthorized facility access: Warning first offense; suspension on repeat
Policy violations (guest policy, reservation rules): Warning; suspension for repeated violations
Disciplinary action documented in audit logs
Member has right to appeal to full Board
### Board Member or Treasurer Violations
Unauthorized data access: Immediate removal from role; investigation by remaining Board members
Misuse of funds: Audit by independent party; referral to appropriate authorities if warranted
Breach of confidentiality: Removal from role; potential membership termination
All violations reported to Principal Officer
