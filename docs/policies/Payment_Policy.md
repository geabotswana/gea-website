# **GEA PAYMENT POLICY**
Gaborone Employee Association
Approved: March 3, 2026
## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes accepted payment methods for GEA membership dues, verification procedures, and treasurer responsibilities. Payment verification is required before membership applications are finalized and member accounts are activated.
## 2. Accepted Payment Methods
GEA accepts membership dues through the following methods, in order of preference:
### Method 1: Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT) to Absa Account
Currency: Botswana Pula (BWP)
Account type: GEA institutional account with Absa Bank Botswana
Account details: [TO BE PROVIDED BY TREASURER]
Payment reference: Member MUST use full name as reference (account holder name not provided to payees)
Exchange rate: Converted at fixed monthly rate (see Section 3)
Proof of payment: Member should retain and provide bank transfer receipt
### Method 2: PayPal
PayPal account: geaboard@gmail.com
Currency: USD (preferred) or BWP
Fees: Member is responsible for PayPal transaction fees (if any)
Proof of payment: PayPal email confirmation sent automatically
### Method 3: State Department Federal Credit Union
Primary method: Zelle transfer to geaboard@gmail.com
Alternative: Member-to-Member code transfer [DETAILS TBD BY TREASURER]
Currency: USD
Proof of payment: Member should retain transfer confirmation
### Method 4: Cash (Last Resort Only)
Cash is NOT preferred and should only be accepted as a last resort
Treasurer must provide written receipt to member
Treasurer must record transaction with date, amount, member name, and witness signature
Currency: Botswana Pula (BWP), converted at fixed monthly rate
## 3. Exchange Rate Determination
**BOARD DECISION (March 3, 2026): Fixed monthly exchange rate published by GEA Board on the first business day of each month. This rate applies to all BWP conversions (EFT payments, cash) for that month. Rate communicated via email to board@geabotswana.org and posted on member portal.**

## 5. Pro-Rating for Mid-Year Membership
Members joining after August 1 pay a pro-rated amount based on the quarter in which they join.
Q1 (August-October): 100% of annual dues
Q2 (November-December): 75% of annual dues
Q3 (January-April): 50% of annual dues
Q4 (May-July): 25% of annual dues
Example: Member joining in December (Q2) pays 75% of annual dues, which carries them through July 31 of the following year.
## 6. Payment Verification Workflow
The treasurer must verify all payments before membership applications are finalized and member accounts are activated. This process is critical to the application workflow.
### Applicant Responsibilities
Submit payment using one of the four methods above
For Absa EFT: Use FULL NAME as payment reference (account holder name not provided to payees)
Retain proof of payment (receipt, email confirmation, bank transfer screenshot, etc.)
Upload or provide proof to treasurer to assist with identification and reconciliation
### Treasurer Responsibilities
Receive and review proofs of payment from applicants
Reconcile proof against bank statements (Absa, PayPal, SDCFE account)
Verify amount paid matches membership type and pro-rating (if applicable)
For Absa payments: Use payment reference (member's full name) to identify payer when account holder name is not visible
For cash: Record transaction with date, amount, member name, and witness signature
Update member payment record in system with verification date and method
Notify applicant of payment verification (triggers final account activation)
Notify board of successful payment verification
## 7. Payment Tracking & Reconciliation
All payments are recorded in the Payment Tracking spreadsheet
Treasurer performs monthly reconciliation of bank statements against recorded payments
Payment records include: payment_id, household_id, amount, method, submission_date, verification_date, verified_by
Treasurer maintains audit trail for all financial transactions
## 8. Annual Dues Collection (Renewal)
Each August (start of new membership year), treasurer:
Sends dues invoices to all active members (email, portal notification, or letter)
Specifies amount due by category and household
Includes deadline: August 31
Includes payment instructions (all four methods)
Includes current month's exchange rate
Receives and verifies payments as submitted
Activates facility access upon verification
Suspends facility access for unpaid members effective September 1
